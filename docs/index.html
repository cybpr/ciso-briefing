Within a 13-day window in April 2026, researcher "Chaotic Eclipse / Nightmare-Eclipse" publicly released three zero-day privilege escalation exploits targeting Microsoft Defender — BlueHammer, RedSun, and UnDefend — after alleging that Microsoft mishandled coordinated disclosure. BlueHammer (CVE-2026-33825, CVSS 7.8) exploits a TOCTOU race condition in Defender's file remediation engine to achieve SYSTEM-level access from an unprivileged account; Microsoft patched it on April 14. RedSun uses a similar technique abusing Defender's cloud-file rollback mechanism and remains fully effective on fully patched April 2026 systems; no CVE or patch exists as of this briefing. UnDefend silently disrupts Defender's signature update pipeline, progressively degrading endpoint protection without generating hard alerts. Huntress confirmed live enterprise exploitation of all three tools beginning April 10, linked to initial access via compromised FortiGate SSL VPN credentials, with a tunneling agent ("BeigeBurrow") deployed for persistence. The combination constitutes a layered attack chain: gain foothold → escalate to SYSTEM → degrade AV → persist and move laterally.

⚡ Immediate Actions: Apply April 2026 Patch Tuesday updates immediately (BlueHammer). Monitor user-writable directories (Downloads, Pictures, Temp) for unsigned binaries. Alert on Defender signature update stalls. Treat any SSL VPN credential compromise as high-severity. Await Microsoft out-of-band patches for RedSun and UnDefend.

Microsoft SharePoint Server Actively Exploited Zero-Day — CVE-2026-32201 (CVSS 6.5) — CISA KEV, Fed Deadline April 28

CVE-2026-32201 is an improper input validation spoofing vulnerability in Microsoft Office SharePoint Server that allows unauthenticated remote attackers to perform spoofing over a network — requiring no user interaction and carrying low attack complexity. The flaw enables confidentiality and integrity impact: attackers can view and modify sensitive information exposed through SharePoint. CISA added it to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog and mandated remediation by April 28 for all federal civilian agencies. Microsoft confirmed active exploitation in the wild but has not disclosed attribution or attack scale; security analysts note it is likely being chained with additional weaknesses, potentially to facilitate double-extortion ransomware operations by enabling data exfiltration from SharePoint document stores prior to encryption. Internet-facing SharePoint Subscription Edition, 2019, and 2016 environments are at immediate risk. April 2026 Patch Tuesday also includes a second SharePoint spoofing flaw (CVE-2026-20945) exploitable via cross-site scripting.

⚡ Immediate Actions: Apply April 2026 SharePoint cumulative update immediately — treat as P1 regardless of CVSS score. Audit internet-facing SharePoint exposure. Monitor for anomalous access to SharePoint document libraries. Review SharePoint audit logs for unauthorized access or content modification.

Anubis RaaS Hits Signature Healthcare Brockton Hospital — 2TB Data Theft, Ambulance Diversions, Care Cancellations

Source: HIPAA Journal / GovInfoSecurity / Comparitech  |  hipaajournal.com  |  Published: April 6–10, 2026  |  MITRE ATT&CK: T1486 (Data Encrypted for Impact), T1567 (Exfiltration Over Web Service), T1490 (Inhibit System Recovery), T1657 (Financial Theft — Ransom Extortion), T1485 (Data Destruction — Wiper Mode)

On April 6, 2026, Signature Healthcare's Brockton Hospital (216-bed, ~70,000 patients/year) detected a ransomware attack that forced it to take electronic health records, its patient portal, and multiple clinical systems offline. The emergency room was placed on divert, ambulances redirected to alternate facilities, chemotherapy infusions at the Greene Cancer Center were cancelled, and retail pharmacies were unable to fill prescriptions. The Anubis RaaS group claimed responsibility on April 9, asserting theft of over 2 terabytes of sensitive patient data, and added the hospital to its dark web leak site with a countdown timer. Anubis is distinguished by an optional file-wipe mode that permanently erases victim files if payment is refused — positioning it closer to destructive malware than traditional ransomware. As of April 21, Signature Healthcare expects recovery to take two weeks, with EHR systems still offline. This attack is part of a documented pattern: the HHS Office for Civil Rights reported 118 large healthcare data breaches affecting 9.6 million individuals in just the first two months of 2026.

⚡ Immediate Actions: Healthcare sector CISOs: validate offline backup integrity and test restoration RTO. Segment clinical networks from corporate IT. Evaluate downtime procedure readiness for EHR outage. Review cyber insurance coverage for business interruption and regulatory fines. Report to HHS/FBI within required HIPAA incident reporting windows.

ShinyHunters Snowflake / Anodot SaaS Supply-Chain Campaign — Rockstar Games, Booking.com & 160+ Orgs Impacted

7.8

ShinyHunters executed a systematic campaign by first compromising Anodot — a SaaS cloud-cost monitoring and analytics platform — and extracting authentication tokens that function as trusted credentials between integrated services. With those tokens, the group accessed connected Snowflake data environments of downstream customers without exploiting any vulnerability in Snowflake itself, as access appeared entirely legitimate. Rockstar Games confirmed a breach (7.54 GB of internal analytics data leaked on April 15 after the ransom deadline lapsed) and Booking.com confirmed unauthorized access to reservation data — names, email addresses, phone numbers, and booking details — for an undisclosed number of customers globally. Booking.com's breach is attributed to Storm-1865, which used the ClickFix phishing technique to compromise hotel partner employees, gaining access to the partner extranet. The Rockstar incident is part of a broader campaign affecting over 160 organizations via Snowflake-connected environments. Stolen Booking.com data is already being weaponized in targeted WhatsApp and email phishing attacks impersonating hotel staff.

⚡ Actions: Audit all third-party SaaS integrations for stored authentication tokens and OAuth grants. Implement SSPM (SaaS Security Posture Management) tooling. Rotate credentials for any analytics, monitoring, or BI platforms with cloud data access. Alert employees on ClickFix social engineering lures. If using Anodot or Snowflake, confirm with vendors whether your environment was assessed.

SAP's April 2026 Patch Day released 19 new security notes, with CVE-2026-27681 as the headline flaw — a near-maximum CVSS 9.9 SQL injection vulnerability in SAP Business Planning and Consolidation (BPC) and SAP Business Warehouse (BW). The vulnerable ABAP program allows a low-privileged authenticated user to upload a file containing arbitrary SQL statements that are subsequently executed by the system, enabling an attacker to read, modify, and delete any data in the BW/BPC database stores without additional user interaction. Onapsis and Pathlock analysts confirm that exploitation could allow extraction of sensitive financial data, alteration of reports and consolidation figures, deletion of database content, and potential full application compromise. While no public proof-of-concept exists and no exploitation in the wild has been confirmed, the low privilege requirement and maximum impact score make this a prime target for both financially motivated and nation-state actors targeting enterprise ERP environments. A secondary high-severity flaw (CVE-2026-34256, CVSS 7.1) in SAP ERP and S/4HANA could allow unauthorized ABAP program execution and logic tampering.
