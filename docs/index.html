Executive Summary

This week delivered a convergence of critical infrastructure vulnerabilities, active zero-day exploitation, and a sustained campaign by the ShinyHunters extortion group that collectively affected hundreds of millions of individuals. The headline threat is a critical cPanel/WHM authentication bypass (CVE-2026-41940) under active exploitation, with some attacks leading to complete wipe of entire websites and backups. In parallel, the Linux "Copy Fail" vulnerability (CVE-2026-31431) enables root privilege escalation across cloud environments and Kubernetes workloads, with a working exploit already in the wild. Progress Software also disclosed a CVSS 9.8 MOVEit Automation authentication bypass (CVE-2026-4670), with exploitation potentially leading to unauthorized access, administrative control, and data exposure. On the threat actor front, Instructure, the company behind the widely-used Canvas LMS, confirmed a data breach — with ShinyHunters claiming responsibility and alleging data from nearly 9,000 educational institutions affecting 275 million individuals, plus billions of private messages. Strategically, AI automation and Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) platforms have fundamentally altered the threat landscape by lowering the capability bar of entry and enabling nation-state actors to automate up to 90% of intrusions. Leadership should treat patching velocity and identity security posture as board-level priorities this week.

A critical authentication bypass vulnerability (CVE-2026-41940) in cPanel is being actively exploited in the wild — and attackers were spotted exploiting it since February 23, likely even earlier before disclosure. The flaw stems from missing authentication for a critical function, allowing unauthenticated remote attackers to gain unauthorized access to the control panel. Successful exploitation grants an attacker control over the cPanel host system, its configurations and databases, and all websites it manages. The Shadowserver Foundation reports 44K unique IPs scanning or running exploits, with approximately 650K IPs hosting exposed cPanel/WHM instances. CISA added CVE-2026-41940 to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on May 1, 2026.

Immediate Action: Update cPanel/WHM to patched versions immediately. Audit all hosted sites and databases for signs of unauthorized modification. Review the cPanel security advisory at support.cpanel.net dated 04-28-2026.

Tracked as CVE-2026-31431 and dubbed "Copy Fail," the flaw resides in the algif_aead module of the Linux kernel's AF_ALG cryptographic subsystem — a logic bug causing improper memory handling during in-place operations. What makes this flaw particularly alarming is its exploitability: a 732-byte Python script is all an unprivileged local user needs to reliably escalate privileges to root. The vulnerability affects virtually all Linux distributions running kernels from 2017 until patched, including Ubuntu 24.04 LTS, Amazon Linux 2023, RHEL 10.1, SUSE 16, Debian, Fedora, and Arch Linux. Successful exploitation leads to full root privilege escalation and could facilitate container breakout, multi-tenant compromise, and lateral movement; its stealth via in-memory-only modification makes it particularly dangerous in cloud, CI/CD, and Kubernetes environments. CISA added CVE-2026-31431 to the KEV catalog on May 1, 2026, with a mandatory remediation deadline of May 15, 2026 for all federal civilian agencies.

Immediate Action: Patch to Linux kernel versions 6.18.22, 6.19.12, or 7.0+. As interim mitigation, block AF_ALG socket creation. Treat any container RCE as potential host compromise. Audit cloud workloads, Kubernetes nodes, and CI/CD runners urgently.

CVE-2026-4670 is an authentication bypass vulnerability exploitable via low-complexity attacks by unauthenticated attackers; CVE-2026-5174, an improper input validation flaw, may allow authenticated attackers to further escalate privileges. Together, the two vulnerabilities may allow remote attackers to gain administrative control of MOVEit Automation instances, accessing credentials stored in tasks, sensitive business data such as reports, payroll, and financial files, and the wider enterprise network. With over 1,400 instances publicly accessible, organizations should place MOVEit Automation behind a VPN or network perimeter control while patching is completed. Whether Cl0p or another group moves first, the 2023 campaign established that the exploitation window for critical MOVEit flaws is measured in hours after public disclosure. Progress states that upgrading via the full installer is the only way to remediate this issue.

Immediate Action: Upgrade to MOVEit Automation 2025.1.5, 2025.0.9, or 2024.1.8 using the full installer (outage required). Block command port interfaces from internet access. Review audit logs for anomalous authentication, privilege changes, or unusual task execution.

The ADT attack was executed via a voice phishing (vishing) campaign that compromised an employee's Okta single sign-on (SSO) credentials, enabling access to the company's Salesforce instance. Have I Been Pwned confirmed the breach exposed the data of 5.5 million people, including unique email addresses, names, dates of birth, phone numbers, physical addresses, and partial government-issued IDs. Within days, educational tech giant Instructure confirmed a separate data breach, with ShinyHunters again claiming responsibility. The group claims to hold over 3.65 terabytes of data from nearly 9,000 educational institutions affecting 275 million individuals, including billions of private messages. ShinyHunters is simultaneously running two distinct attack playbooks: vishing for SSO credentials and exploiting misconfigured Salesforce Experience Cloud environments. Student data is protected under FERPA, and the updated COPPA rule effective April 22, 2026, tightens consent and breach notification requirements for children's data.

Immediate Action: Deploy phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2/passkeys) on all SSO gateways. Audit Salesforce Experience Cloud guest user permissions and Organization-Wide Defaults. Conduct emergency vishing awareness training for help desk and IT staff. Review all API keys and token access to SaaS platforms.
