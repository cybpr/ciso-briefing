🔴 ACT NOW ShinyHunters Breach of Canvas LMS (Instructure) — Largest Educational Data Breach on Record
RPS: 10.0
Source Wikipedia / TechCrunch / Malwarebytes Published 6–11 May 2026
MITRE ATT&CK T1190 (Exploit Public-Facing App) · T1657 (Financial Theft/Extortion) · T1567 (Exfiltration Over Web Service) · T1486 (Data Encrypted for Impact)
Severity (S) 10 / 10 Exploitation (E) 10 / 10 — Confirmed; ransom paid
Exposure (X) 10 / 10 — 8,809 institutions, ~275M records, 41% of US higher ed

The hack is considered the largest educational security breach on record as of May 8, 2026, due to its unprecedented global scale, affecting 8,809 universities, educational ministries, and other institutions worldwide — with particular impact in the United States, where Canvas is used by 41% of higher education institutions. The ShinyHunters group claimed to have stolen 3.65 terabytes of data (approximately 275 million records), including private messages exchanged between students and teachers. Despite Instructure's claim that the situation had been resolved, on May 7 Canvas was hacked again — its login page was replaced with a ransomware message by ShinyHunters, who threatened to release the data unless their ransom was paid by end of May 12. On May 11, Instructure issued an apology for their lack of transparency and announced they had reached an agreement with the hackers for an undisclosed amount and that the compromised data was destroyed. Security experts noted this breach "follows a clear pattern" of attackers moving up the data supply chain to target platforms sitting underneath thousands of institutions at once, rather than targeting individual campuses.

⚡ Decision Point: Enterprises with Canvas or other Instructure integrations must immediately: (1) issue staff/student phishing warnings leveraging breach data; (2) audit what data was shared with Instructure under data processing agreements; (3) assess FERPA/GDPR notification obligations. The breach is resolved per vendor, but follow-on phishing and credential stuffing attacks using the stolen PII remain a live threat.
🔴 ACT NOW CVE-2026-41940 — cPanel/WHM Authentication Bypass: Mass Exploitation, Ransomware & Nation-State Espionage
RPS: 9.25
Source Help Net Security / The Hacker News / CISA KEV Published 30 Apr – 11 May 2026
CVE CVE-2026-41940   CVSS 9.8 (Critical) CWE CWE-306 (Missing Auth for Critical Function)
MITRE ATT&CK T1190 (Exploit Public-Facing App) · T1003 (OS Credential Dumping) · T1059 (Command/Scripting Interpreter) · T1486 (Data Encrypted for Impact) · T1133 (External Remote Services)
Severity (S) 9 / 10 Exploitation (E) 10 / 10 — Mass exploitation confirmed since Feb 23
Exposure (X) 8 / 10 — 650K+ exposed cPanel instances; 44K+ IPs compromised

A critical authentication bypass vulnerability (CVE-2026-41940) in cPanel is being exploited in the wild — and attackers had been abusing it since at least February 23, well before the April 28 public advisory. Successful exploitation grants an attacker control over the cPanel host system, its configurations and databases, and all websites it manages, with approximately 1.5 million cPanel instances exposed to the internet. Multi-actor exploitation has now led to disrupted websites, ransomware deployment, and targeted attacks — including a Go-based Linux encryptor that encrypts files and appends the ".sorry" extension, then drops a ransom note directing victims via Tox. Over 2,000 unique IPs have been attributed to exploitation activity enabling Filemanager backdoor deployment, credential theft, and persistent access. Separate nation-state espionage activity leveraging CVE-2026-41940, detected May 2, 2026, targeted government and military domains in the Philippines and Laos, as well as MSPs and hosting providers.

⚡ Decision Point: If your organisation or any vendor/hosting provider runs cPanel/WHM, treat this as a potential active breach. Apply patches immediately; block ports 2083/2087/2095/2096 at the firewall. Hunt for .sorry-suffixed files and anomalous login events from unexpected IPs since February 23, 2026. Rebuild from clean backups if IOCs are present.
🔴 ACT NOW CVE-2026-0300 — Palo Alto PAN-OS Unauthenticated RCE (CVSS 9.3): Perimeter Firewall Zero-Day Under Active Exploitation
RPS: 9.0
Source Palo Alto Advisory / Help Net Security / Rapid7 / CISA KEV Published 6 May 2026
CVE CVE-2026-0300   CVSS 9.3 (Critical) / 8.7 (if restricted) CWE CWE-787 (Out-of-Bounds Write)
MITRE ATT&CK T1190 (Exploit Public-Facing App) · T1068 (Exploitation for Privilege Escalation) · T1133 (External Remote Services) · T1021 (Remote Services) · T1059 (Command/Scripting Interpreter)
Severity (S) 9 / 10 Exploitation (E) 9 / 10 — Confirmed active exploitation (CL-STA-1132); CISA KEV
Exposure (X) 9 / 10 — 225,000 internet-facing PAN-OS instances (Shodan); NO PATCH until 13 May

CVE-2026-0300 allows unauthenticated attackers to achieve remote code execution with root privileges, affecting the User-ID Authentication Portal on PA-Series and VM-Series firewalls — with active exploitation confirmed, particularly where the portal is exposed to untrusted networks or the public internet. An unauthenticated remote attacker can exploit this vulnerability by sending specially crafted packets, achieving arbitrary code execution with root privileges on the affected firewall — no authentication or user interaction is required. No patches are currently available; fixed versions are expected to begin rolling out on May 13, 2026, with additional releases through May 28. Shadowserver Foundation has identified more than 5,800 PAN-OS VM-Series firewalls exposed to the public internet, and CISA has placed the bug in its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog with a May 9, 2026 deadline for federal agencies. Successful exploitation grants a root shell on the firewall, enabling complete control: traffic interception, configuration changes, backdoor installation, or lateral movement into protected networks.
