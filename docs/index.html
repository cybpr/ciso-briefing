A critical vulnerability (CVE-2026-0300) affecting Palo Alto Networks firewalls is being actively exploited; it is a buffer overflow vulnerability in the User-ID Authentication Portal (Captive Portal) service of PAN-OS software. The vulnerability can be exploited by unauthenticated attackers to execute arbitrary code with root privileges on PA-Series and VM-Series firewalls by sending specially crafted packets. Palo Alto Networks disclosed that threat actors may have attempted to exploit this flaw as early as April 9, 2026; the CVSS score is 9.3/8.7. As of May 7, 2026, Palo Alto Networks believes these attacks are likely the work of state-sponsored threat actors. Full PAN-OS patches are staged for release around May 13 and May 28; organizations should immediately restrict Authentication Portal access to trusted zones or disable it if not required.

The Iranian state-sponsored hacking group MuddyWater has been attributed to a ransomware attack in what has been described as a "false flag" operation; the attack was observed by Rapid7 in early 2026 and appeared to be consistent with a RaaS group operating under the Chaos brand, but evidence points to it being a targeted state-backed attack. All enterprises using Microsoft Teams with external access enabled should audit tenant settings and enforce strict external communication policies immediately.
